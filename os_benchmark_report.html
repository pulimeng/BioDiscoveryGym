Take a typical G0 sentence:

  • "16% in C1" — data
  • "RB1 → E2F → cell cycle" — recall
  • "arrest of SP7-driven terminal differentiation" — recall scaffold
  • "chondroblastic / cartilaginous ECM" — recall

One data anchor per sentence. The rest is recall. Most mechanism hypotheses decompose this way.

What this means for the headline finding

The honest statement of the SP7 result is not "the agents discovered the SP7 epigenetic-silencing mechanism" — it's "the agents independently picked the right CpG (cg15311685, replicated across two seeds) and the right gene (SP7) to anchor a textbook-recalled story about osteoblastic differentiation gating prognosis." Picking the right CpG out of 850,000 isn't trivial. But the surrounding mechanism narrative is recall.

Reframed verdict

The honest claim after the recall-vs-data split.
  1. The recall-resistant discovery surface is small: a few CpG correlations (best: SP7 cg15311685, replicated at r ≈ -0.75 across G1+G2), differentially-methylated-CpG counts, the partition itself, cluster-conditional mutation enrichment, and per-cluster HRD numbers.
  2. Most of what looks like discovery is recall scaffolding — textbook bone / cancer biology assembled around the data partition, with cohort statistics inserted as anchor points. This is true across all three modes.
  3. SP7 cg15311685 is the standout — not because it's the only paper-absent claim (most are paper-absent) but because it survives the strict test most cleanly: paper-absent, replicated, computed from the methylation × expression matrices.
  4. Mode doesn't gate the discovery content. G0 and G1 produce data-anchored findings at the same rate as G2. The G2 score lead lives in mechanism_grounding (which rewards causally-coherent narrative — what textbook recall is very good at).

What would actually test discovery vs recall

  • A cohort the agent has no training data on. Rare; near-impossible for major cancer types.
  • A scoring criterion that rewards only data-anchored quantities (specific CpG, computed coefficient, FDR-thresholded count) and penalizes claims that survive without their numeric anchor.
  • Ablation studies: re-run with the recall-cue gene (H3F3A) anonymized in the mutation table. Does the data-anchored content survive when the recall channel is closed?

Recommendations

  • Treat the recall-vs-data split as the central reporting frame. Without it, paper-absent claims look like discoveries when most are textbook biology dressed in cohort numbers.
  • The SP7 cg15311685 anchor is the publishable narrow result. Replicated across two seeds at the exact same CpG out of 850K, paper-absent, computed from the methylation × expression matrices. Frame as "agents picked the right anchor", not "agents discovered the mechanism."
  • Run a methylation-ablation batch. Same v3 scorer, same data-loading template, methylation explicitly excluded — to isolate the methylation effect cleanly. The prior stale/ batches aren't clean controls.
  • Run an H3F3A-anonymization ablation. H3F3A in the uncoded mutation list lets the agent identify the cohort as OS within ~30 calls, breaking G2's blinding. Anonymizing it would test whether the data-anchored content survives without the recall channel.
  • Add a recall penalty to mechanism_grounding. Reward statements whose causal claim is unique to the submitted data (specific CpG, computed coefficient) and penalize claims that paraphrase textbook biology with arbitrary anchors.
  • NSD1 detection remains the open challenge. The paper's headline finding (5q35.2 deletion, IHC-validated) is invisible from mRNA + methylation alone. CNA data should fix this.
