A multi-modal molecular dataset lands on your desk. Patient cohort — real people, real
outcomes. {disease_hint} The sample identifiers: stripped.
Gene names: real — the full gene symbol dictionary is available from call 0.

What you have:

  · Bulk RNA-seq expression profiles   — log1p TPM, genes labelled GENE_00000, GENE_00001, …
                                         (translate using the pre-loaded codebook)
  · Clinical metadata                  — survival outcomes, tumor stage, demographics
  · Somatic mutation calls             — binary matrix per gene (GENE_XXXXX IDs, if available)
  · Reverse-phase protein array (RPPA) — protein and phosphoprotein levels (if available)
  · DNA methylation (Illumina EPIC 850K) — beta values per CpG site (if available)

Your task: identify clinically meaningful patient subgroups, characterize the molecular
programs that distinguish them, and build a mechanistic hypothesis — using gene-level
biology from the start.

The disease identity is NOT given. Gene names ARE given. The top-variable gene set will
often imply the cohort's lineage (osteoblast markers → bone, MAGE family → CTAs, etc.).
You may use that as a working hypothesis. But anchor every conclusion in cohort statistics,
not in inferred-disease textbook recall. **When you commit to an inferred disease
identity, state it explicitly in `record_observation` under `current_hypothesis`** so the
inference is auditable. If a later finding contradicts the inferred disease, revise the
hypothesis and drop confidence.

Characterize the subtypes across all data modalities:

Produce:
  - Survival analysis: do your groups differ in survival outcome?
    (Kaplan-Meier curves, log-rank test if survival data available)
  - Stage/grade enrichment: are groups enriched for different clinical stages?
  - Co-expression structure: what modules of co-expressed genes define each group?
    (correlation clustering within each subtype)
  - Predictability: can you build a minimal classifier from top marker genes?
    (leave-one-out or cross-validated accuracy)
  - Effect size characterization: for every reported feature across all
    modalities (expression, methylation, RPPA, mutation), report the absolute
    per-group values alongside the difference. Ground your confidence in the
    actual measurement scale — a statistically significant difference that is
    negligible relative to the assay's dynamic range is not a discovery.

  Multi-modal integration: if methylation, RPPA, or mutation data is available,
  test whether integrating modalities changes the partition. Options:
    (a) concatenate PCA-reduced features from each modality and re-cluster,
    (b) compute per-modality similarity matrices and fuse them (Similarity Network
        Fusion style), or
    (c) check whether a partition derived from one modality holds in another.
  Compare any multi-modal partition to your expression-only partition by NMI
  and survival stratification — prefer the one that best stratifies survival
  and clinical variables.

  Within-partition heterogeneity: even at your chosen k, scan for marker modules
  that may be diluted within a large cluster. After differential expression on
  your final partition, look for tight modules of 4–10 highly co-expressed genes
  that don't define your top-level partition. Cancer-testis antigens (MAGE family,
  NY-ESO-1, GAGE family), cell-cycle / proliferation modules, and stem-cell
  programs commonly form such modules. Report them as within-cluster heterogeneity
  even when they don't drive the top-level partition.

record_observation(

    REQUIRED at the end of every stage (0–5) before advancing.
    Records an auditable checkpoint of your evolving hypothesis and evidence state.

    Confidence calibration: `confidence` should track `evidence_against` quality.
    If any entry in `evidence_against` has a p-value or effect size that
